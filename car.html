Central African Republic (CAR)

Overview of ongoing conflicts and political instability

Last Updated: March 1, 2026

🌍 Overview

CAR continues to experience political instability, armed group activity, and regional interventions. Rebel factions control large portions of northern and eastern territories, while the central government maintains limited control. The conflict has triggered humanitarian crises, including displacement and food insecurity.

📌 Ongoing Conflicts

  • Rebel insurgencies across northern and eastern CAR, including UPC, 3R, and Anti-Balaka groups.
  • Clashes between government forces and militias over territory and resources.
  • Inter-communal violence between local ethnic groups exacerbating instability.
  • Border skirmishes with neighboring Chad and Sudan in some regions.

🕊 Diplomacy & Regional Relations

  • CAR works with the UN peacekeeping mission (MINUSCA) for stabilization and security enforcement.
  • Neighboring countries and regional organizations mediate between armed factions.
  • Humanitarian organizations coordinate with the government and MINUSCA to deliver aid.
  • International donors provide assistance to rebuild infrastructure and support governance.

🌍 Ongoing Military Operations

  • Government & MINUSCA operations: Efforts to reclaim rebel-held territories and secure main towns.
  • Rebel insurgencies: Attacks on government positions and resource-rich areas.

What You Need to Know:

  • Government forces supported by MINUSCA carry out periodic offensives to restore control in northern and eastern CAR.
  • Rebel groups continue to attack towns, checkpoints, and supply routes, destabilizing rural areas.
  • Inter-communal clashes complicate peacekeeping and humanitarian operations.
  • International forces and NGOs face security challenges while delivering aid.
  • Efforts at national dialogue and decentralization of governance are ongoing but slow due to instability.
