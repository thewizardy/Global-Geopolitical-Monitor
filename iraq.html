🌍 Overview

Iraq continues to face insurgent activity, sectarian violence, and political instability. The Iraqi government, supported by international partners, is battling remnants of ISIS while addressing internal political tensions, militia influence, and economic challenges. Cross-border tensions with Syria and regional actors remain significant.

📌 Ongoing Conflicts

  • ISIS insurgent attacks in western and northern Iraq.
  • Sporadic clashes between government forces and local militias.
  • Sectarian tensions occasionally lead to localized violence.
  • Cross-border activity with Syria impacts security in border provinces.

🕊 Diplomacy & Regional Relations

  • Collaboration with US-led coalition to combat ISIS remnants.
  • Engagement with Iran, Turkey, and neighboring countries to manage border security and influence militias.
  • UN and international organizations assist in humanitarian relief and stabilization efforts.
  • Political negotiations within Iraq aim to manage sectarian balance and reform governance.

🌍 Ongoing Military Operations

  • Iraqi Government Operations: Targeting ISIS strongholds in western and northern provinces.
  • Coalition Airstrikes: International coalition conducts targeted air operations against remaining insurgents.
  • Militia Activity: Local militias engage in both security enforcement and occasional clashes with rival groups.

What You Need to Know:

  • ISIS continues low-level insurgency, particularly in desert and mountainous areas.
  • Security forces work to stabilize urban centers while managing militia influence.
  • Cross-border movements from Syria influence attacks and require monitoring.
  • Political instability, corruption, and sectarian divides impact governance and security effectiveness.
  • Humanitarian access and reconstruction efforts face challenges due to insecurity and political tension.
