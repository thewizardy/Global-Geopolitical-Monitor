📌 Overview

Colombia continues to face armed conflict involving insurgent groups, paramilitaries, and organized crime networks. Violence and territorial disputes persist in rural and urban areas, impacting civilians and government forces.

⚔ Military & Security Actions

  • National military and police forces conduct operations against insurgent groups and cartels.
  • Counter-narcotics and anti-crime operations targeting major trafficking networks.
  • Special security measures in conflict zones to protect civilians and infrastructure.

🤝 Diplomatic Engagements

  • Regional cooperation with neighboring countries to reduce cross-border insurgent movement.
  • Engagement with international organizations to support peacebuilding and humanitarian efforts.

🌍 Ongoing Military Operations

  • Counter-Insurgent Operations: Military campaigns targeting ELN, FARC dissidents, and organized crime groups.

What You Need to Know:

  • ELN and FARC dissident factions remain active in several departments including Arauca, Chocó, and Norte de Santander.
  • Government forces conduct targeted strikes, patrols, and intelligence-led operations.
  • Anti-narcotics operations focus on dismantling drug production and trafficking networks.
  • Civilians are affected by violence, displacement, and disruptions to daily life.
  • International observers monitor human rights and provide aid in conflict-affected areas.
