🌍 Overview

Yemen remains engulfed in a protracted civil war, with the Houthi movement controlling the north and the internationally recognized government based in the south. Regional powers, including Saudi Arabia and the UAE, are heavily involved through military and diplomatic means. The conflict has created one of the world’s worst humanitarian crises, with millions displaced and widespread food insecurity.

📌 Ongoing Conflicts

  • Houthi insurgency against the Yemeni government and allied forces.
  • Airstrikes and ground operations by the Saudi-led coalition targeting Houthi positions.
  • Local tribal clashes and militia activity in southern Yemen.
  • Attacks on infrastructure, ports, and oil facilities impacting civilians and trade.

🕊 Diplomacy & Regional Relations

  • UN-led peace talks continue to negotiate ceasefires and humanitarian access.
  • Saudi Arabia, UAE, and Iran are major regional actors influencing the conflict.
  • International aid agencies work to provide relief despite security challenges.
  • Efforts to stabilize governance in southern Yemen continue under the Yemeni government and local authorities.

🌍 Ongoing Military Operations

  • Saudi-led Coalition Operations: Airstrikes and border operations targeting Houthi military infrastructure.
  • Houthi Counteroffensives: Missile attacks, drone strikes, and ground offensives in northern Yemen.

What You Need to Know:

  • The Houthis maintain control over northern Yemen, including Sanaa, and launch frequent missile and drone attacks.
  • Saudi-led coalition strikes target Houthi strongholds but often affect civilian areas, worsening humanitarian conditions.
  • Southern Yemen sees sporadic militia and tribal violence complicating governance.
  • Humanitarian organizations face access restrictions and security threats in conflict zones.
  • International diplomacy, including UN-sponsored negotiations, seeks to implement ceasefires and facilitate aid delivery.
