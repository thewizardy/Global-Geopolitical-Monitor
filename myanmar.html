📌 Overview

Myanmar continues to face internal armed conflict following the 2021 military coup, with clashes between the junta and ethnic armed organizations. Civilian populations are heavily impacted, with widespread displacement and human rights violations.

⚔ Military & Security Actions

  • Myanmar military (Tatmadaw) conducts operations against ethnic armed groups and pro-democracy militias.
  • Ethnic insurgents engage in guerrilla tactics across border and rural regions.
  • Security operations target both armed opposition and civilian populations in contested areas.

🤝 Diplomatic Engagements

  • ASEAN and UN efforts to mediate between the military junta and opposition groups continue with limited progress.
  • International organizations provide humanitarian aid and monitor human rights violations.

🌍 Ongoing Military Operations

  • Internal Conflict Operations: Military campaigns and insurgent clashes ongoing in Kachin, Shan, and Kayin states, as well as urban areas under junta control.

What You Need to Know:

  • Clashes continue between the Myanmar military and ethnic armed organizations, causing civilian casualties and displacement.
  • Pro-democracy militias engage in guerrilla operations against military positions.
  • Humanitarian access is limited in conflict zones, exacerbating food and healthcare shortages.
  • International monitoring reports ongoing human rights violations by multiple parties.
  • Diplomatic pressure is being applied through ASEAN, UN, and other global actors, though significant progress remains limited.
