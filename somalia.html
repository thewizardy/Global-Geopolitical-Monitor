🌍 Overview

Somalia faces persistent political instability, insurgent activity, and humanitarian crises. Al-Shabaab and other militant groups continue operations across southern and central regions. Regional actors, including the African Union Mission in Somalia (AMISOM), maintain security efforts, but attacks and bombings remain frequent.

📌 Ongoing Conflicts

  • Insurgency by Al-Shabaab in southern and central Somalia.
  • Inter-clan clashes affecting control of key towns and districts.
  • Maritime piracy and attacks along the Somali coast.
  • Regional interventions by Ethiopia and Kenya in border areas.

🕊 Diplomacy & Regional Relations

  • Somalia engages with the African Union for peacekeeping and stabilization efforts.
  • Ongoing collaboration with the United Nations for humanitarian support and counter-terrorism measures.
  • Tense relations with neighboring states over cross-border security operations.
  • Attempts to rebuild federal government structures and promote national reconciliation.

🌍 Ongoing Military Operations

  • Al-Shabaab Insurgency: Active operations against Somali government and AMISOM forces.

What You Need to Know:

  • Al-Shabaab continues attacks in Mogadishu and southern regions, targeting government officials, security forces, and civilians.
  • AMISOM and Somali National Army conduct joint operations to dismantle insurgent cells.
  • Kenya and Ethiopia occasionally intervene in border regions to counter cross-border threats.
  • Insurgent attacks include bombings, ambushes, and assassinations, disrupting local governance and humanitarian aid delivery.
  • Humanitarian organizations face challenges accessing conflict-affected areas due to security risks.
