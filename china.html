📌 Overview

China is a rising global superpower with expanding military capability, economic reach, and diplomatic influence. Its strategic objectives include Indo-Pacific dominance, reunification policy regarding Taiwan, economic expansion through global infrastructure projects, and reshaping global institutions toward a multipolar framework.

⚔ External Military Actions & Deployments

  • Regular naval and air force patrols in the South China Sea.
  • Military pressure operations near Taiwan.
  • Border force posture along the India-China frontier.
  • Naval expansion into the Western Pacific and Indian Ocean.
  • Participation in joint exercises with strategic partners.
  • Overseas base presence (e.g., Djibouti).

🤝 Major Diplomatic Engagements

  • Belt and Road Initiative partnerships across Asia, Africa, and Europe.
  • Strategic alignment with Russia.
  • BRICS economic coordination.
  • Regional diplomacy within ASEAN frameworks.
  • Economic engagement across the Global South.

🏠 Internal Security & Domestic Actions

  • Internal stability and surveillance systems.
  • Cybersecurity and information control policies.
  • Defense modernization programs.
  • Strategic industrial and technological development initiatives.

💰 Strategic Economic Tools

  • Global manufacturing dominance.
  • Technology export growth and semiconductor development.
  • State-backed infrastructure investments abroad.
  • Currency internationalization efforts.

🛡 Military Capability

  • Nuclear arsenal with expanding capabilities.
  • Rapid naval expansion and aircraft carrier development.
  • Advanced missile and anti-access/area denial systems.
  • Growing cyber and space capabilities.

📊 Strategic Tension Index

Global Engagement Level: Very High

Regional Conflict Risk (Indo-Pacific): High
